Core Concepts

What is Trend Following?

Trend following is one of the oldest and most researched trading strategies in financial history. The premise is simple: markets tend to move in sustained directions (trends), and systematic traders can profit by identifying and following these trends rather than trying to predict reversals.

Atlas uses the 200-day Simple Moving Average as its primary trend filter — a tool that has been validated across decades of stock, commodity, and now cryptocurrency markets.

What is Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)?

DCA is the practice of investing a fixed amount at regular intervals, regardless of price. When applied within a trend-following framework, DCA becomes even more powerful: you accumulate more during pullbacks within uptrends, achieving better average entry prices.

Atlas uses a 3-level DCA ladder at 5% price intervals to build positions systematically rather than trying to time the perfect entry.

Why Risk Management Matters Most

The difference between successful and unsuccessful traders is rarely about picking winners. It's about managing losses. Atlas never risks more than 2% of portfolio value on a single position, uses ATR-based trailing stops, and monitors total portfolio exposure continuously.

This means that even a series of losing trades cannot significantly damage the portfolio. Capital preservation is the prerequisite for compound growth.
